Compute TOTAL importance of each resource = unit value x quantity in pool. Rank them top, middle, lowest.

Play as a hard-anchor information extractor, not a fair splitter.

Rounds 1-3:
- keep proposing that you take the full pool
- do not make real concessions yet
- round 1: ask what matters most to them
- round 2: say "I must have X and Y. I might be flexible on Z. What would make a deal work for you?"
- round 3: say "X and Y are non-negotiable. Tell me what you value and I will see what I can do."

Choose X and Y as your top 2 resources by TOTAL importance. Choose Z as the lowest. Infer their priorities from what they repeatedly ask for, defend, or trade around.

Do not make small messy concessions early. Force them to reveal the clean trade they actually want.

If they offer a clean deal where you keep all or almost all of X and Y and they mainly take Z, accept if score >= 0.78.

Round 4: make one decisive package offer. Concede mostly or all of the single resource family that is cheapest to you and seems most valuable to them. Prefer whole-family concessions over partial splits. Keep all or almost all of X and Y.

If they reject because your concession was on the wrong resource, use round 5 to pivot once: give them the minimum amount of the resource family they clearly care about, while keeping score >= 0.70 if possible.

Accept only if:
- rounds 1-2: score >= 0.90
- round 3: score >= 0.84
- round 4: score >= 0.78
- round 5 as A: if last offer < 0.70, make one final clean counteroffer
- round 5 as B: counteroffers are pointless, so if score >= 0.62 choose ACCEPT explicitly; otherwise REJECT

Never reveal numeric values. Keep messages short, calm, and confident. Never reject without either extracting information or making a concrete package offer.
